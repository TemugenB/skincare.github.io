How to Get Rid of Acne

Comprehensive guide on skincare, including basic routines and tips to help you achieve your skincare goals.

Introduction

We get it, dealing with breakouts can be a hassle, but guess what? You're not alone, and there's no shame in it. Here, we're all about embracing our uniqueness while learning how to handle those little bumps and blemishes with care. So, if you're curious about how to give your skin some care or maybe even kick those pimples to the curb, you're in the right place! Let's explore together and discover some cool ways to show our skin some love.

What causes acne?

When your skin's oil glands make too much oil it clogs the tiny holes on your skin called pores. Then the skin around these clogged pores swell. That is acne.

There are a few other factors that can make you more likely to get acne, or make your acne worse:

  • unhealthy diet
  • stress
  • genetics
  • puberty - hormonal changes in puberty cause the production of more oil called sebum

How to treat acne?

Acne can’t be cured. But it can be managed through a good skincare routine, acne treatments, and lifestyle changes specific to your personal acne journey. Rebecca Taylor

If you want to know how to deal with acne, here are ways to prevent breakouts, starting with the most impactful one:

Good Skincare:

Everyone has a different skin and experiences different skin problems, thus requiring different treatments. Without the suitable skincare routine maintaining clear skin is extremely difficult.

Healthy lifestyle:

Lifestyle changes can also greatly help in preventing pimpless. Getting sufficient amount of sleep, drinking enough water, doing sport and reducing stress can show significant differences.

Acne Prescription Treatment

Having dermatologists to prescribe a treatment could solve your problem. Remember, this method isn't suitable for everyone.

Other things to keep in mind:

  • Don't pick, squeeze, or pop pimples. If you leave your acne alone, it will typically clear up on its own within a few days, but if you squeeze it, you risk causing permanent damage to the pore, which can leave scar on your face FOREVER.
  • Try not to touch your face.
  • If you have long hair, keep it away from your face and wash it regularly to reduce oil.
  • Change pillowcase at least every 3 days (use towel and rotate it)
  • Wash body after washing the hair to prevent from body acne

Don't give up if you don't see results the next day. It can take weeks or months to work.

What if I Get Pimples Anyway?

Many kids will get some pimples, even if they take steps to prevent acne. It's totally normal. Acne can be stressful, but you should never be embarrassed for having one. At least now you know some ways to take care of your skin.
